Some Academic History

I went to Shanghai Foreign Language School until 2015. I then went to Cranbrook Schools at Bloomfield Hills, Michigan, where I found my initial academic interests: Cognitive Science. I therefore applied and began my college in Psychology.

Still, CMU was renowned for its computer science program. After taking some introductory courses to programming (why not), I found greater pleasure in coding. I decided to switch to the School of Computer Science, and here I am.

Acadmic Interests

My current interests are in distributed systems, including distributed storage, failure-tolerance protocols, and distributed computing. I have worked on the open source Ray project mentored by Hao Zhang, where I developed ray collective feature. I also worked primarily on distributed storage systems during my internship at ByteDance.
